7 Proven Steps to Boost Your Credit Score in 30 Days

Sarah Mitchell
February 5, 2026 • 8 min read
Credit Tips

Your credit score is the key to unlocking better financial opportunities — from lower interest rates to premium credit cards.

Step 1: Review Your Credit Reports for Errors

Pull reports from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. Studies show 1 in 5 consumers have errors that lower scores.

Pro Tip

Use AnnualCreditReport.com and look for unfamiliar accounts, incorrect balances, or outdated negatives.

Step 2: Dispute Inaccurate Items

Under the FCRA, bureaus must investigate disputes within 30 days. AI tools can automate this process.

Step 3: Lower Your Credit Utilization

  • Pay balances aggressively
  • Request credit limit increases
  • Spread charges across cards
  • Pay before statement date

Step 4: Become an Authorized User

Adding yourself to a well-aged account can instantly improve utilization and account age.

Step 5: Pay All Bills on Time

Payment history accounts for 35% of your FICO score.

Warning

One missed payment can drop your score 100+ points and remain for 7 years.

Step 6: Avoid Opening Too Many New Accounts

Each hard inquiry can reduce your score by 5–10 points.

Step 7: Monitor Your Progress Weekly

Use credit monitoring tools to track score changes and alerts.
